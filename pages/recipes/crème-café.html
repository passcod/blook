Crème au café

Mandatory ingredients

  • 1L milk
  • 65g maïzena (fine corn flour)
  • 150g sugar
  • Coffee in some form

Optional ingredients

  • Spices: vanilla, cinnamon, clove, or to taste
  • 50g to 200g liquid cream, as you want
  • 30ml amaretto (almond liqueur)

Method

  1. Place milk, maïzena, sugar, and vanilla in Thermomix, set to 90°C, 18 minutes, speed 4.
  2. After 10 minutes, reduce speed to 2.
  3. Meanwhile, make the coffee. If you have instant coffee, use 30g to 50g of that, depending on how strong you want the cream. If you don’t, or you prefer to make your own, go about how you’d make a long black or a double shot of espresso. If you’re like me, and you have neither instant coffee nor coffee machine or any kind of coffee-related contraption, but you do have ground coffee beans for some reason, make tradie coffee:
    1. Spoon some 2 tsp grounds into a (clean) cup.
    2. (Add your other dry spices, to taste.)
    3. Pour some boiled water on top.
    4. Wait about 10 minutes.
    5. Pour the top of the coffee into another cup, avoiding the yuck soggy grains at the bottom.
    For the purposes of our recipe, though, pour into a small saucepan instead, and bring to a vigorous boil for about 8 minutes, to reduce it.
  4. Add the coffee, amaretto, and cream to the cream, and give it a whirl at speed 3 for at least 30 seconds.
  5. Leave it running while you prep your ramekins or bolinettes, then pour.
  6. Clean your thermomix immediately (better now than later, trust me).
  7. Once your creams have cooled down enough, fridge all but one. Eat that one warm. You know you want to.
  8. They’ll probably never set all the way like jelly does, but after two hours they’ll be good eatingstuff, for you or yours.
